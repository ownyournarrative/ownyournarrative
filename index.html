About Me

Hi, I’m Kaartik Issar — a high-distinction Computer Science graduate from the University of Toronto, consistently ranked among the top computer science programs in the world.

My journey with code began at the age of 13, when I built my first website out of pure curiosity — a small portal for a school project that looked awful by today’s standards, but made me feel like a magician. I was hooked.

As I grew older, I realized that code wasn’t just about logic or syntax — it was a medium for storytelling. In a world where first impressions happen online, the way you present yourself digitally can make all the difference.

That realization changed my life.

During university, I applied for an internship at a company where hundreds of students had stronger grades and shinier résumés than mine. But I had something most didn’t — a personal website. Instead of just a static PDF, I shared a sleek, personalized site that showcased my work, my projects, and even a short video introducing myself. Within 24 hours, the hiring manager replied: “This stood out. We’d love to chat.”

That one link helped me land not just one, but multiple roles — even when I wasn’t the “obvious” candidate on paper. Since then, I’ve built 100+ personal websites for professionals across industries — from designers to product managers, engineers to authors. Every one of them experienced the same lightbulb moment:

“This is me, online — the way I want to be seen.”

A personal website isn’t just a digital business card. It’s a signal. It says you care. It says you’re thoughtful, intentional, and professional. It gives you control over your story — instead of leaving it up to a random LinkedIn snippet or Google search result.

So if you’ve ever felt like your résumé doesn’t capture the full you — or you want to stand out in a world of endless scrolling and sameness — I’d love to help you craft a digital home that’s unmistakably yours.

Let’s build it.
